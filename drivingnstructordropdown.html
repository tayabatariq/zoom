What Are The Requirements To Become A Driving Instructor?

To become an Approved Driving Instructor (ADI) in the UK, you must meet specific personal requirements and pass three qualifying tests administered by the Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency (DVSA).

Personal and Legal Requirements
  • Be 21 years old or over.
  • Hold a full, clean UK or EU driving licence for at least three years.
  • Have fewer than 6 penalty points on your licence.
  • Have not been banned from driving in the last four years.
  • Pass an enhanced Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) check (must be a new one specifically for the ADI application).
  • Demonstrate that you are a "fit and proper" person, meaning your personal and professional conduct meets the DVSA's standards.
  • Be able to read a standard number plate from 27.5 metres away, with corrective lenses if necessary.
  • Apply to the ADI register through the DVSA: Apply on GOV.UK

Do I need to sit tests to become a Driving Instructor?

The ADI qualification process involves three exams, which you must pass in order within a two-year period after passing the first test. You can find detailed information on the official GOV.UK guide to becoming a driving instructor.

Format: This test has two sections:

  • Multiple-choice: 100 questions covering the Highway Code, instructional techniques, and the law. You must score at least 85% overall and a minimum of 80% in each of the four categories.
  • Hazard perception: You will watch 14 video clips and must score at least 57 out of 75 by clicking to identify developing hazards.

Attempts: You have an unlimited number of attempts to pass this test.

Format: This practical test takes about one hour and assesses your expert-level driving skills. It includes:

  • An eyesight check.
  • "Show me, tell me" vehicle safety questions.
  • A demonstration of expert driving ability and manoeuvres.

Attempts: You can have a maximum of three attempts. If you fail all three, you must wait two years after passing Part 1 before you can restart the process.

Format: This test evaluates your ability to teach a learner driver effectively during a one-hour lesson. The examiner observes you teaching a pupil on a pre-planned topic.

  • After passing Part 2, you can apply for a six-month trainee licence (pink badge) to gain practical teaching experience before taking this final test.
  • This allows you to earn money while gaining experience teaching real students.
  • You must do at least 40 hours of training before applying.
  • Many instructors use this to train under a franchise or school.

Attempts: You have a maximum of three attempts.

Additional Skills and Registration

Beyond passing the tests, successful ADIs are patient, excellent communicators, and have strong time-management skills. After passing all three parts, you must register with the DVSA within one year to legally charge for driving lessons.

Your registration must be renewed every four years, which includes another DBS check and a standards check to ensure you maintain your teaching abilities.
